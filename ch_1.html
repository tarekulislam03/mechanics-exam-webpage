Core concepts

Rigid Body
A body in which the distance between any two particles remains constant under the action of forces. Deformation is neglected.
Equilibrium
A body is in equilibrium when the net force AND net moment about any point are both zero. Body remains at rest or moves with constant velocity.
Concurrent Forces
All forces pass through a single common point. Their resultant is found by vector addition. Moment condition is automatically satisfied.
Coplanar Forces
All forces lie in the same plane (usually XY). Three equilibrium equations apply: ΣFx=0, ΣFy=0, ΣM=0.
Resultant Force
A single force that produces the same effect as all given forces combined. R = √(ΣFx² + ΣFy²), angle θ = tan⁻¹(ΣFy / ΣFx).
Moment of a Force
Tendency of a force to cause rotation about a point. M = F × d, where d is the perpendicular distance from the point to the line of action.
Varignon's Theorem
The moment of a resultant force about any point equals the algebraic sum of moments of individual component forces about the same point.
Couple
Two equal, opposite, parallel, non-collinear forces. Net force = 0, but moment ≠ 0. Moment = F × d (always constant about any point).
Free Body Diagram (FBD)
A diagram showing the body isolated from its surroundings with all external forces (applied loads + reactions) acting on it clearly labeled.
Components in Space (3D)
Force F has components Fx = F·cosα, Fy = F·cosβ, Fz = F·cosγ where α, β, γ are angles with X, Y, Z axes. cos²α + cos²β + cos²γ = 1.
Direction Cosines
l = cosα, m = cosβ, n = cosγ. Unit vector: λ̂ = (l î + m ĵ + n k̂). Always satisfies l² + m² + n² = 1.

2D Coplanar System

Resultant magnitude and direction
R = √[ (ΣFx)² + (ΣFy)² ]    θ = tan⁻¹(ΣFy / ΣFx)
Equations of equilibrium (coplanar)
ΣFx = 0  |  ΣFy = 0  |  ΣM_A = 0
Moment of a force about point O
M = F × d  (d = perpendicular distance)
Couple moment
C = F × d  (constant everywhere in the plane)

3D Space Forces

Force components in 3D
Fx = F·cosα  |  Fy = F·cosβ  |  Fz = F·cosγ
Direction cosine identity
cos²α + cos²β + cos²γ = 1
Resultant in 3D
R = √(Fx² + Fy² + Fz²)
6 Equations of Equilibrium (3D rigid body)
ΣFx=0  ΣFy=0  ΣFz=0  |  ΣMx=0  ΣMy=0  ΣMz=0

Lami's Theorem

For 3 concurrent coplanar forces in equilibrium
F₁/sin α₁ = F₂/sin α₂ = F₃/sin α₃
α₁, α₂, α₃ = angles OPPOSITE to forces F₁, F₂, F₃ respectively

Varignon's Theorem

M_R (about O) = Σ(M_i about O) = F₁d₁ ± F₂d₂ ± ...

Example solved problems

Q1. Two forces 100 N (at 30°) and 150 N (at 60°) act at a point. Find the resultant.
ΣFx = 100 cos30° + 150 cos60° = 100(0.866) + 150(0.5) = 86.6 + 75 = 161.6 N
ΣFy = 100 sin30° + 150 sin60° = 100(0.5) + 150(0.866) = 50 + 129.9 = 179.9 N
R = √(161.6² + 179.9²) = √(26114 + 32364) = √58478 = 241.8 N
θ = tan⁻¹(179.9/161.6) = tan⁻¹(1.113) = 48.1° with X-axis
Q2. A beam AB of 6 m is simply supported at A and B. A point load of 12 kN acts at 2 m from A. Find support reactions.
Taking moment about A: R_B × 6 = 12 × 2
R_B = 24/6 = 4 kN
ΣFy = 0: R_A + R_B = 12 → R_A = 12 − 4 = 8 kN
Check ΣM_B = R_A×6 − 12×4 = 48 − 48 = 0 ✓
Q3. A force of 200 N acts along a line making angles α=60°, β=45° with X and Y axes. Find components and angle with Z-axis.
cos²α + cos²β + cos²γ = 1 → cos²60° + cos²45° + cos²γ = 1
0.25 + 0.5 + cos²γ = 1 → cos²γ = 0.25 → cosγ = 0.5 → γ = 60°
Fx = 200 × cos60° = 100 N
Fy = 200 × cos45° = 141.4 N
Fz = 200 × cos60° = 100 N
Q4. Three concurrent forces 300 N, 400 N, 500 N are in equilibrium. Find the angles between the forces using Lami's Theorem.
By Lami's: 300/sinα₁ = 400/sinα₂ = 500/sinα₃ and α₁+α₂+α₃ = 360°
Let k = common ratio. sinα₁ = 300/k, sinα₂ = 400/k, sinα₃ = 500/k
This is a 3-4-5 right triangle set → α₃ = 180° − 90° = angle opposite 500 N ≈ 180°
sinα₁ = 300/500 × sin(opposite) → Using sine rule: α₁ ≈ 126.87°, α₂ ≈ 143.13°, α₃ ≈ 90°
Check: 126.87 + 143.13 + 90 = 360° ✓
Q5. A force of 60 N acts at point (3, 4) m. Find its moment about the origin O.
Perpendicular distance from O = √(3² + 4²) = √25 = 5 m
If the 60 N force acts at the point along a specific direction, M = F × d_perp
Assuming force acts perpendicular to the position vector:
M = 60 × 5 = 300 N·m

Lami's Theorem — complete breakdown

Statement
If three concurrent coplanar forces keep a body in equilibrium, each force is proportional to the sine of the angle between the other two forces.
Formula
F₁/sinα₁ = F₂/sinα₂ = F₃/sinα₃
Key condition
Only 3 forces, all concurrent, all coplanar, and the body must be in equilibrium. α₁ + α₂ + α₃ = 360°
Important note
Each angle is the angle OPPOSITE to the respective force (i.e., the angle between the other two forces), NOT the angle between that force and another.
Proof basis
Derived from the Sine Rule of a triangle formed by drawing the three force vectors head-to-tail (closed triangle of forces in equilibrium).

Typical exam numerical (Lami's)

A 500 N weight hangs from two strings making 30° and 45° with the vertical. Find tensions T₁ and T₂.
Three forces: T₁ (along string 1), T₂ (along string 2), W=500 N (downward)
Angle between T₂ and W = 180°−45° = 135°, Angle between T₁ and W = 180°−30° = 150°
Angle between T₁ and T₂ = 360°−135°−150° = 75°
By Lami's: T₁/sin135° = T₂/sin150° = 500/sin75°
500/sin75° = 500/0.9659 = 517.6
T₁ = 517.6 × sin135° = 517.6 × 0.7071 = 366 N
T₂ = 517.6 × sin150° = 517.6 × 0.5 = 258.8 N

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using Lami's for more than 3 forces — it only applies to exactly 3 concurrent forces.
  • Taking the angle between a force and an adjacent force instead of the angle opposite to it.
  • Forgetting that the three angles must sum to 360°, not 180°.
  • Not converting the weight/load into the correct force direction when drawing FBD.
